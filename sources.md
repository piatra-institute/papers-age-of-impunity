# Sources

The frozen bibliography. Each entry as it appears in the paper's `## References`, with a one-line provenance note. Every in-text `et al.` maps to a co-authored entry below (Alstadsaeter, Johannesen and Zucman written out in full in-text).

## Bibliography

Alstadsæter, A., Johannesen, N., and Zucman, G. (2019). Tax evasion and inequality. *American Economic Review*, 109(6), 2073–2103. — offshore evasion concentrated at the top; automatic information exchange as an evidence-stage shock.

Arendt, H. (1973). *The Origins of Totalitarianism* (new ed.). Harcourt Brace Jovanovich. — organized lying, factual truth, and the erosion of the ground institutions stand on.

Becker, G. S. (1968). Crime and punishment: an economic approach. *Journal of Political Economy*, 76(2), 169–217. — deterrence as an expected-sanction inequality; the chain product and B > P*S + R.

Cohen, J. E. (2019). *Between Truth and Power: The Legal Constructions of Informational Capitalism*. Oxford University Press. — control over data, classification, and platforms as a governing legal power.

Crawford, K. (2021). *Atlas of AI: Power, Politics, and the Planetary Costs of Artificial Intelligence*. Yale University Press. — AI as an extraction of power, not a neutral tool.

Eubanks, V. (2018). *Automating Inequality: How High-Tech Tools Profile, Police, and Punish the Poor*. St. Martin's Press. — the chain run downward against the weak (welfare, policing).

Foucault, M. (1977). *Discipline and Punish: The Birth of the Prison* (A. Sheridan, Trans.). Pantheon Books. — surveillance and discipline; legibility below.

International Consortium of Investigative Journalists. (2024). *Panama Papers and FinCEN Files investigations*. ICIJ. — cross-border investigation; the 2024 Panama trial acquitting all 28 defendants (exposure without consequence).

Levitsky, S., and Ziblatt, D. (2018). *How Democracies Die*. Crown. — erosion through capture of referees rather than coups.

Mutz, D. C. (2018). Status threat, not economic hardship, explains the 2016 presidential vote. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 115(19), E4330–E4339. — the multicausal, older motives that defeat the strong thesis.

Noble, S. U. (2018). *Algorithms of Oppression: How Search Engines Reinforce Racism*. New York University Press. — algorithmic classification as discrimination.

O'Neil, C. (2016). *Weapons of Math Destruction: How Big Data Increases Inequality and Threatens Democracy*. Crown. — opaque scoring systems turned on subjects.

Power, M. (1997). *The Audit Society: Rituals of Verification*. Oxford University Press. — verification can ritualize and detach from substantive outcomes.

Scott, J. C. (1998). *Seeing Like a State: How Certain Schemes to Improve the Human Condition Have Failed*. Yale University Press. — legibility as ambivalent capacity, not intrinsic justice.

Transparency International. (2025). *Corruption Perceptions Index 2024*. Transparency International. — most jurisdictions below the midpoint; the conversion-institution deterioration.

V-Dem Institute. (2025). *Democracy Report 2025*. University of Gothenburg, V-Dem Institute. — autocratizing states and the share of population affected.

World Justice Project. (2024). *Rule of Law Index 2024*. World Justice Project. — declines in oversight, executive checks, and expression across most countries.

Zuboff, S. (2019). *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*. PublicAffairs. — behavioural data as a new instrument of power.
