## Findings

- [T1] Deterrence as an expected-sanction inequality: misconduct is attractive while expected benefit exceeds the probability of punishment times its severity — Becker (1968), *JPE* 76(2). The chain product P = prod(stage probs) and the B > P*S + R condition (§2).
- [T1] Legibility is politically ambivalent: the standardization that lets a state tax, enforce standards, or detect fraud is the same that lets it classify, displace, and discipline — Scott (1998), *Seeing Like a State*. Grounds the rights-symmetry result and the "opacity above, legibility below" point (§5, §6).
- [T2] Audit and verification can improve oversight but also become ritualized and detached from outcomes — Power (1997), *The Audit Society*. The conversion stages are not automatic (§4).
- [T1] Status threat, not personal economic hardship, best explains the 2016 vote — Mutz (2018), *PNAS* 115(19). Used to defeat the strong (anticipatory-counterrevolution) thesis: the movements have older, multicausal motives (§1).
- [T1] Automatic international exchange of bank information substantially reduces offshore evasion; evasion is concentrated at the top — Alstadsaeter, Johannesen and Zucman (2019), *AER* 109(6). Anchors the evidence-stage saturation (the early stages can be driven toward 1) (§2).
- [T4] The Panama Papers / FinCEN Files: cross-border investigation across dozens of countries (FinCEN 88), and the 2024 Panama money-laundering trial acquitting all 28 defendants — ICIJ (2024). The exposure-without-consequence pattern: discovery succeeds, enforcement fails (§5).
- [T3] Institutional deterioration is concentrated in the conversion institutions: dozens of autocratizing states (V-Dem), declines in oversight, executive checks, and free expression in most countries (World Justice Project), most jurisdictions below the corruption midpoint (Transparency International) — V-Dem Institute (2025); World Justice Project (2024); Transparency International (2025). The model's predicted locus of capture (§5).
- [T2] Democracies erode through the capture of referees (courts, prosecutors, election bodies) rather than coups; visible unpunished wrongdoing corrodes the norms institutions run on — Levitsky and Ziblatt (2018); Arendt (1973) on organized lying and factual truth. The legitimacy cost of legible impunity (§5).
- [T2] Algorithmic systems turned on subjects automate inequality and punishment (predictive policing, welfare denial, search bias) — Foucault (1977); Eubanks (2018); O'Neil (2016); Noble (2018); Crawford (2021); Zuboff (2019); Cohen (2019). The chain run downward; legibility below (§6).
- [Inference] The model formalizes the seed's verdict ("evidence scarcity to adjudication scarcity") and the historical regularity ("there has never been a final accountability medium") as a conservation law: a product is bounded by its weakest controllable link, and a self-protective elite always has one, so any improvement confined to the uncapturable (evidence) stages is absorbed at the capturable (institutional) one. This predicts attacks on courts/prosecutors over documents WITHOUT requiring the strong thesis's anticipatory motive.
